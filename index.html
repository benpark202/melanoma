Officially coined in 1838 by British pathologist Sir Robert Carswel, Melanoma is a severe form of skin cancer that forms from melanocytes in the epidermis, which become damaged and produce pigment seen as moles. Melanoma develops after excess exposure to ultraviolet (UV) light. While all types of UV light are harmful to humans, UV-B rays are particularly responsible for the majority of skin cancer cases. Melanoma is typically treatable under 99% of cases if detected early, typically through surgical removal (Mohs) or radiation.
